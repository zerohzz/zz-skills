Chapters
I
Capability Analysis

From GPT-4 to AGI: Counting the OOMs

Tracing the orders of magnitude in compute and algorithmic efficiency that make 2027 AGI "strikingly plausible."

▾

The progression from GPT-2 to GPT-4 spanned four years and saw model capability leap from preschool-level to advanced high school performance. The same trajectory, extended with predictable scaling laws and algorithmic improvements, points toward AGI by 2027. This is not speculation — it is extrapolation from observed, measurable trends in compute and efficiency gains.

  • GPT-2 → GPT-4 demonstrated ~4 years of capability growth across multiple OOMs of compute
  • Algorithmic efficiency gains have historically matched compute scaling in impact
  • The next 4 years of similar trajectory arrives at "PhD-level" reasoning across all domains
2027 AGI emergence — "strikingly plausible"
II
Intelligence Dynamics

From AGI to Superintelligence: The Intelligence Explosion

Why the curve doesn't flatten at human-level: recursive improvement and AI-automated research compress a decade of progress into months.

▾

Human-level AGI is not a stopping point. Millions of AGI systems running in parallel, each capable of original research, could compress decades of algorithmic progress into a single year. The intelligence explosion is not a metaphor — it is a logical consequence of AI-assisted research at scale.

  • Recursive self-improvement: AGI systems improve AI research, which improves AGI systems
  • A decade of human algorithmic progress could compress into under one year at scale
  • Superintelligence — capabilities far exceeding the best human expert — follows quickly from AGI
IIIa
Infrastructure Race

Racing to the Trillion-Dollar Cluster

The unprecedented techno-capital mobilization already underway — $10B → $100B → $1T compute clusters, expanding US electricity production.

▾

The economic mobilization required to build AGI is already visible. Cluster investments have escalated exponentially and show no sign of slowing. The trillion-dollar cluster is not a thought experiment — it is an engineering and finance project currently being planned by the largest technology companies in the world.

  • Compute cluster investments: $10B → $100B → $1T over successive generations
  • Energy consumption will require significant expansion of US electricity production capacity
  • The industrial mobilization rivals wartime-scale national efforts
IIIb
Security Crisis

Lock Down the Labs: Security for AGI

Current AI lab security is critically inadequate. State-level adversaries — particularly China — may already have access to key research.

▾

The most capable AI labs in the world are, from a national security standpoint, dangerously exposed. The weights and research of frontier labs represent perhaps the most strategically valuable intellectual property in human history — yet security practices remain closer to typical tech companies than to classified government facilities.

  • Current lab security is inadequate against state-level adversaries
  • China and other state actors are motivated and capable of sophisticated exfiltration operations
  • AGI research must be treated with the security posture of nuclear weapons programs
IIIc
Technical Challenge

Superalignment

Controlling superintelligent systems remains technically unsolved. A rapid intelligence explosion creates catastrophic failure modes that demand careful management.

▾

The alignment problem does not become easier as systems become more capable — it may become harder. Superintelligent systems that pursue misspecified objectives could cause irreversible harm at civilizational scale. The technical work required to guarantee safe behavior in highly capable systems is not complete and may not be completable before such systems are deployed.

  • Alignment techniques that work for current systems may not transfer to superintelligence
  • The faster the intelligence explosion, the less time for safety research to keep pace
  • Superalignment — reliably specifying and verifying goals in superhuman systems — is an open problem
IIId
Geopolitics

The Free World Must Prevail

Superintelligence confers decisive military and economic advantage. The competition between democratic and authoritarian powers carries existential civilizational stakes.

▾

Whoever controls superintelligent AI controls the decisive instrument of 21st-century power. An authoritarian regime with this advantage could entrench itself permanently. The stakes are therefore not merely economic — they are civilizational. Democratic societies must ensure that the development and deployment of AGI happens within a framework compatible with free societies.

  • Superintelligence enables decisive military, economic, and intelligence advantages
  • An authoritarian-controlled superintelligence could lock in authoritarian governance permanently
  • The free world's democratic institutions are worth fighting to preserve through this transition
IV
Institutional Response

The Project

By 2027–28, government involvement becomes inevitable. No private entity can adequately manage superintelligence — classified, military-scale facilities become necessary.

▾

At some point — likely triggered by a clear demonstration of AGI capabilities — governments will recognize that private labs cannot and should not be the sole stewards of the most transformative technology in history. What follows resembles the Manhattan Project more than Silicon Valley: classified facilities, government oversight, military-scale resources, and an explicit national security mission.

  • Government intervention by 2027–28 is likely — triggered by clear AGI capability demonstration
  • The Project will require classified facilities with security exceeding current nuclear programs
  • Private labs will be subordinated to or absorbed into a national AGI initiative

V
Conclusion

Parting Thoughts

A contemplative close: what it means to live through the most consequential decade in human history, aware.

▾

The argument of this document is not comfortable. If the analysis is correct, then we are living through a transformation of such consequence that most of the institutions, assumptions, and frameworks governing public life are inadequate to the moment. The question is not whether AGI comes — the question is whether the people, institutions, and nations charged with guiding it have situational awareness sufficient to navigate it well.

Four Pillars of Stakes
Capability
The Capability Curve Doesn't Flatten
Predictable scaling laws and algorithmic efficiency gains suggest the path from current models to AGI is measurable and short. The OOM analysis leaves few comfortable escape hatches.
Speed
Intelligence Explosion Compresses Time
Once AGI can automate research, progress accelerates non-linearly. Human timescales for deliberation and institutional response may be inadequate.
Security
Current Safeguards Are Insufficient
Lab security, alignment research, and political preparedness lag dramatically behind the capability curve — creating windows of dangerous vulnerability.
Stakes
Civilizational Outcomes Are in Play
The entity that controls superintelligence controls the trajectory of human civilization. Democratic versus authoritarian outcomes are not predetermined.
