## 1. XHS Platform Overview

Xiaohongshu (小红书, "Little Red Book") is a content-sharing platform where users discover products, lifestyles, travel, food, and ideas through short-form image carousels accompanied by brief captions. What works on XHS:

- **Authentic voice** — readers trust personal experience over polished corporate writing. First-person, specific, honest.
- **Visual-first thinking** — each slide must work as a standalone image; text is secondary to the visual impression.
- **Community-driven** — content that invites engagement ("你怎么看？"/"What do you think?") performs better than broadcast-style writing.
- **Practical value** — "I learned this and it changed how I work" outperforms "Here is some information about X."
- **Discovery framing** — titles and covers are the hook; readers swipe past anything that looks generic.

The goal of rewriting for XHS is not translation — it is **adaptation**: extracting the source article's key insights and reimagining them in the authentic, warm, experience-sharing voice that XHS readers expect.

## 2. Title Guidelines

The cover title is the most important text in the carousel. It determines whether someone taps to read.

**Rules:**
- Under 20 Chinese characters (or ~40 English characters for English-mode slides)
- Avoid generic openers: 不要用 "关于…", "浅谈…", "探讨…"
- Formats that work well:

| Format | Example |
|---|---|
| Question | 为什么我用 AI 一天就做完了整个网站？ |
| Bold claim | 我找到了最好用的笔记方法 |
| Number-led hook | 5个让效率翻倍的 AI 工具 |
| Contrast / surprise | 放弃了番茄工作法之后，我反而更专注了 |
| Personal milestone | 坚持早起100天，我的生活变了什么 |

**What to avoid:**
- Clickbait with no payoff (destroys trust)
- Titles that could apply to any article (too generic)
- Titles over 20 characters — they get cut off in the feed

## 3. Paragraph Style

XHS readers scroll quickly. Dense paragraphs are skipped.

**Rules:**
- **2–3 sentences maximum** per paragraph
- Hard line breaks between paragraphs (not indentation)
- One idea per paragraph — if a paragraph has two ideas, split it
- Contractions and colloquialisms are fine: "真的", "超级", "绝了", "有点", "其实"
- Rhetorical questions add rhythm: "你有没有遇到过这种情况？"

**Emoji placement:**
- 1–2 emojis per paragraph, placed at natural pauses — after a key point, at the end of a sentence
- Use emojis that reinforce meaning, not random decoration
- Avoid emoji overload — it reads as spam

**Good example:**
```
这个方法改变了我处理邮件的方式。✉️ 以前我每天花两个小时收件箱，现在只需要20分钟。

关键是批处理：把所有邮件集中在固定时段处理，而不是随时查看通知。📵 这样你的注意力就不会被不断打断。
```

**Poor example:**
```
邮件处理是现代工作者面临的重要挑战之一，其对生产力的影响不可忽视。本文将探讨一种经过验证的批处理方法，该方法通过将邮件处理集中在特定时间段来减少注意力的碎片化。
```

## 4. Cover Design

The cover is slide 1 and the first thing readers see in the feed. Three elements matter:

**Emoji selection:**
- One emoji that captures the article's core mood or topic
- Should be expressive, not literal — if the article is about focus, 🎯 works better than 📧
- Avoid: ✅ ❌ 📌 (overused on XHS, signal low-effort content)
- Good choices: emojis that are slightly unexpected but immediately make sense

**Title:**
- Apply the title guidelines from section 2 above
- The cover title can be slightly shorter than the `xhs_content.md` title if needed for visual fit

**Subtitle:**
- One punchy line — the payoff of the title
- 10–15 characters ideally; hard limit 25 characters
- Should answer "what will I get from this?" or complete the title's thought
- Examples:
  - Title: 为什么我用 AI 一天做完了整个网站 → Subtitle: 工具清单 + 完整流程
  - Title: 我找到了最好用的笔记方法 → Subtitle: 再也不怕忘事了

## 5. Slide Content Distribution

Each content slide = one clear topic. When planning how to assign content across slides:

**Principles:**
- **One idea per slide** — do not cram two unrelated points onto a single slide
- **Progressive flow** — slides should build on each other; a reader who skips one should still follow the rest
- **Balanced length** — aim for similar word counts per content slide; avoid one sparse slide next to one overflowing slide
- **Strong openers** — the first content slide (slide 2) should be the most compelling hook after the cover

**Content distribution patterns:**

| Article type | Suggested structure |
|---|---|
| How-to / tutorial | Slide 2: Why this matters → Slides 3–7: Steps 1–N → Slide 8: Common mistakes → Slide 9: Summary |
| Opinion / argument | Slide 2: The claim → Slides 3–6: Supporting evidence → Slide 7: Counterpoint → Slide 8: Conclusion |
| List / roundup | Slide 2: Intro + criteria → Slides 3–8: Items 1–6 → Slide 9: Favorites + CTA |
| Personal story | Slide 2: Before state → Slides 3–6: What happened → Slide 7: Turning point → Slide 8–9: After + lessons |
| Concept explainer | Slide 2: The problem → Slides 3–5: The concept → Slide 6–7: Examples → Slide 8: How to apply → Slide 9: Summary |

## 6. Hashtag Strategy

Hashtags drive discovery on XHS. They appear at the end of the caption (not in slide content).

**Rules:**
- 3–5 hashtags per post
- Format: `#话题名#` (with closing `#`)
- Mix broad + specific: one high-traffic broad tag + two–three niche tags

**Selection strategy:**
1. One broad category tag (e.g., `#效率工具#`, `#职场技能#`, `#生活方式#`)
2. One topic-specific tag (e.g., `#AI工作流#`, `#笔记方法#`, `#晨间习惯#`)
3. One audience or intent tag (e.g., `#打工人必看#`, `#学生党#`, `#程序员#`)
4. Optional: one trend or seasonal tag if relevant

**Examples by topic:**

| Article topic | Hashtags |
|---|---|
| AI productivity tools | `#效率工具#` `#AI工具#` `#打工人必看#` `#工作技巧#` |
| Morning routine | `#早起打卡#` `#生活方式#` `#习惯养成#` `#自律#` |
| Technical tutorial | `#编程学习#` `#程序员#` `#技术干货#` `#前端开发#` |
| Book summary | `#读书笔记#` `#书单推荐#` `#知识分享#` |

## 7. Tone Guide

XHS tone is authentic, personal, and experience-grounded. It sounds like a knowledgeable friend sharing what they learned, not a brand communicating at you.

**Markers of good XHS tone:**
- First-person ("我发现…", "我用了半年…", "说真的…")
- Specific details ("用了3个月", "每天早上8点", "试过5种方法之后")
- Honest admission of past failure or confusion before the solution
- Genuine enthusiasm that doesn't feel scripted
- Inviting reader participation at the end ("你们怎么看？", "有没有人也有这个问题？")

**Markers of bad XHS tone (avoid):**
- Corporate or academic phrasing: "本文旨在", "综上所述", "不可否认"
- Passive voice with no personal stake
- Buzzwords without substance: "赋能", "颠覆", "生态"
- Overly hedged language: "可能", "或许", "也许" in every sentence
- All-caps or excessive exclamation marks (looks spammy)

**Adapting English blog tone:**
- English blogs are often analytical and structured — XHS prefers narrative and personal
- "This tool has three main features" → "用了这个工具之后，我的工作方式变了"
- Lead with the human experience, then the detail; English blogs often do the reverse

## 8. Chinese Typography

For Chinese-language slides:

**Quotation marks:**
- Use `「」` for inline quotes (not `""` or `''`)
- Use `『』` for nested quotes within a quote
- Example: 他说「这是我见过最好的方法」

**Spacing:**
- No space between Chinese characters
- Add a half-width space between Chinese and Latin/numbers: `用了 3 个月`, `AI 工具`, `GPT-4 模型`
- End sentences with `。` not `.` for Chinese text

**Character limits per block:**
- Heading (h2): 10–16 characters recommended; hard limit 20
- Heading (h3): 8–14 characters recommended
- Paragraph: 30–60 characters per sentence; 2–3 sentences per paragraph
- List item: 15–25 characters per item; 3–6 items per list

**Emphasis:**
- Use **加粗** sparingly — for the single most important phrase per slide
- Avoid underlining (looks like a hyperlink)
- Color emphasis is handled by the theme; do not add inline color styles to text

## 9. Language Adaptation

When the source is an English blog post that needs to be adapted to Chinese XHS format:

### Translation principles

1. **Translate meaning, not words** — rewrite the sentence in how a Chinese speaker would naturally express the same idea
2. **Cut academic hedging** — English academic writing hedges constantly; XHS readers want directness
3. **Localize examples** — where the original uses a Western cultural reference, replace or add a Chinese-familiar parallel
4. **Shorten aggressively** — English explanations often run 50% longer than needed; XHS slides have limited space

### Common patterns

| English pattern | Chinese XHS adaptation |
|---|---|
| "Research has shown that X leads to Y" | "你有没有发现，X 之后往往会 Y？" |
| "There are three steps to..." | "三步搞定：" |
| "Many people struggle with..." | "很多人都有这个问题，包括以前的我" |
| "In conclusion, X is important because..." | "说到底，X 真的太重要了——[specific reason]" |
| "You should consider using X" | "强烈推荐试试 X，用完你就懂了" |

### Structure shift

English blog posts are typically structured as: **intro → argument → evidence → conclusion**

XHS carousel structure should be: **hook → problem → insight → application → takeaway**

When adapting, map:
- Intro → compress into cover title/subtitle
- Background/context → 1 slide max, lead with the problem not the history
- Main argument sections → 1 slide each, reframe as personal insights
- Conclusion → final slide with clear, actionable takeaway + invitation to engage

## 10. Before / After Example

### Source (English blog paragraph)

> One of the most overlooked productivity techniques is time blocking — the practice of scheduling specific tasks into dedicated time slots rather than working from an undifferentiated to-do list. Research from Cal Newport and others suggests that this approach significantly reduces context-switching overhead and enables deeper focus states. Unlike reactive task management, time blocking forces you to make explicit decisions about your priorities before the day begins.

### After XHS adaptation (Chinese)

**Slide heading:** 为什么我戒掉了待办清单

**Slide content:**
```
我用了两年的待办清单，结果每天忙到晚上还是觉得什么都没做完。😩

后来换了「时间块」方法——每项任务提前分配专属时间段，不是看心情干活，而是按计划行动。

效果完全不同。🎯 因为你在一天开始前就做了最重要的决定：什么事情值得你几个小时的专注。
```

### Key transformations applied

| Original | Transformation |
|---|---|
| "Research from Cal Newport and others suggests" | Removed — replaced with personal experience framing |
| "significantly reduces context-switching overhead" | Replaced with felt experience: "忙到晚上还是觉得什么都没做完" |
| Passive/analytical tone | Shifted to first-person narrative with emotional arc |
| Long single paragraph | Split into 3 short paragraphs with natural emoji placement |
| Formal conclusion | Ended with an insight the reader can immediately apply |
